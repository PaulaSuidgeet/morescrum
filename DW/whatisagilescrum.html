What is Agile Scrum?

Scrum is a management framework that helps teams work together. Much like any team training for a big sports game, Scrum encourages teams to learn through experiences, self-organize while working on a problem, and reflect on their wins and losses to continuously improve. Scrum describes a set of roles, meetings/events, rules and artifacts that work in concert to help team structure and manage their work. The teams, usually consisting of about seven people each, are responsible for creating and adapting their process within this framework and are self-organizing and cross-functional. The team model in Scrum is designed to optimize flexibility, creativity, and productivity and always consists of a Product Owner, the Development Team, and a Scrum Master.

While the Scrum is most frequently used by software development teams, its principles and lessons can be applied to all kinds of teamwork. This is one of the reasons Scrum is so popular.
